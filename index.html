Operational Philosophy

Transparency

I believe in being human and candid. If a technical solution isn't worth the cost, I say so. I do not sell "features"; I verify utility.

Build Quality

I prioritize hardware that is built to last, not just hardware that is built to sell. I look at capacitors, PCB traces, and thermal paths before I look at branding.

Efficiency

Balancing professional ventures with a dedicated family schedule, ensuring high output and focused delivery during operational hours.

Fiscal Realism

I don't recommend the newest technology for the sake of novelty; I recommend the technology that is proven to pay for itself through reliability and low maintenance.

Documentation Accountability

Clear, jargon-free records are the only way to ensure a system is actually understood by the owner, not just "working" for the moment.

Sovereign Infrastructure

Minimizing reliance on opaque third-party providers. If you don't control the hardware and the data, you don't own the system.
